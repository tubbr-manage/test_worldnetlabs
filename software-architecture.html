Software Architecture

WNL engineers are well versed in the latest software architectures and we constantly add new experts in fields as they emerge. Good software architecture is key to managing complex distributed systems, event-driven systems, and big data. Advanced software architectures are needed for resilient designs, choosing between monoliths and microservices, edge computing, hybrid cloud for microservices and containers, agile frameworks that incorporate DevSecOps, hyper-Automation and effective deployments of AIOps to name a few.

Software-enabled systems are driving major advancements in collaborative work environments, healthcare, online learning and social networking. These systems must incorporate personal needs, population diversity, privacy and physical safety. They must energy-aware and make spaces, such as industries, cities, and houses smart and sustainable. Taking these critical considerations into account will require advanced software-enabled systems and drive new architectural considerations. One of the most critical success factors for the design of these systems is the level of abstraction that the software architecture incorporates. The architecture of a software system describes its static and dynamic structures in terms of its software components, their inter-connection, and properties as well the rationale explaining its design and evolution. A prescriptive architecture can be an artifact devised by an architect to decide about what design to choose among alternatives, as well as an artifact dynamically composed by a running system that uses it to make decisions about selecting alternative configurations to deal with runtime change.

WNL engineers are adept at adaptable, evolvable, verifiable software design that meets functional and quality requirements. Linking engineering requirements in software architectural design, analysis, conformance checking and evaluation stages; using architecture description languages and meta-models; architecture verification and validation while developing software; architectural concerns of self-adaptive and autonomic systems; uncertainty management in architecture-based adaptation; risk mitigation of self-adaptive systems; identifying architectural patterns, styles and tactics, reference architectures, viewpoints and views; virtualization and agile constructs are some major areas that go into software architectural decisions. Scientists need to factor in cultural, economic, business, social and managerial aspects into their software architecture. Software architectures need to adapt to different areas such as the cloud/edge-cloud, big data, blockchain, digital twins, cyber-physical systems, IoT, autonomous systems, systems of systems, energy-aware software.

We at WNL are committed to building the right architecture for your application requirements.
