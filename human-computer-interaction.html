Enabling the next generation of interactive solutions

Most companies start building products, tools and systems driven by a new idea or technology. Focusing on users comes as an afterthought. User Centric Design (UCD) is still a buzzword and not a core element of the design process. This is partially because UCD requires skills that are not commonly available in design teams. Companies that have put UCD at the core have launched very successful and differentiated products that have taken over the market.

Here at World Net Labs, our experts start every project with UCD as the foundation. We combine Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and User-Experience (UX) to understand customer’s behaviours, motivations, and goals and then structure every step of the design to deliver the best customer experience.

HCI emphasizes design, evaluation, and use of information and communication technologies with an explicit goal to deliver the best user experience, task performance, and quality of life.

The user, the task, the interface, and the context are the four elements that determine the usability for a given product or service. “Usability” and HCI are interchangeable. HCI combined with user experience (UX) and user interface (UI) creates more intuitive technology to better serve the needs of the global citizens.

Through qualitative and quantitative research methods, the WorldNet Labs HCI experts test new techniques to delight, inspire, and make life easier for all users.


Voice-guided interfaces are one example of HCI. Without worrying about typing errors or getting distracted while working to search something on the web, voice activated assistants allow us to ask what we want as if talking to a friend. These interfaces make acquiring information a casual and convenient exercise. HCI experimentation has refined this technology and helped designers gain valuable insight into the differences between how people speak to each other versus how they speak to technology.


AR/VR/MR is a fast-emerging HCI technology for a broad range of applications. Using Virtual Reality (VR) product developers can create virtual environment and models in which the user can perform their tasks as if they were in a physical reality. Combined with Digital Twin models, VR is revolutionizing medical, smart city, manufacturing, entertainment and many other industries, shifting how users interact intuitively with products in real life situations.
